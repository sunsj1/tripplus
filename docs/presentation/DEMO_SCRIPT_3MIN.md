# TripPlus — 3 Minute Demo Script

> A ready-to-read walkthrough for live demos, investor pitches, and onboarding
> sessions. Structured as a single user story so every smart feature surfaces
> naturally instead of being listed.
>
> **Total runtime:** 3 minutes 20 seconds at a relaxed pace.
> **Companion file:** [`SMART_FEATURES.md`](../SMART_FEATURES.md) — the complete
> feature inventory if you need to dive deeper on any point.

---

## The setup (15 seconds)

> "Meet Priya. She drives a petrol Hyundai Creta and she's planning a Friday
> evening trip from Mumbai to Pune with her family — about 150 kilometres,
> three hours. She has TripPlus on her phone.
>
> Watch how the app earns its place from the moment she opens it."

---

## Step 1 — Planning the trip (35 seconds)

> "Priya opens the **Plan tab**. The 'From' field is already pre-filled with
> **'Current location'** — no typing. She types 'Pune' in the destination and
> taps **Analyze Route**.
>
> In about 4 seconds, three things appear:
>
> First, a **Trip Overview** card showing ETA — about 3 hours 15 minutes —
> toll estimate around ₹350, fuel cost ₹1,200, and **traffic level: Moderate**.
> Every estimate is labelled '~ estimates only' so she knows these aren't
> promises.
>
> Second, a **Weather on Route** strip shows three cards — Origin, Midway,
> Destination. Origin clear, Midway light showers, Destination clear. The
> amber '1 caution' badge tells her to expect rain near Khandala.
>
> Third, the **Smart Trip Timeline** lays out the journey vertically with
> distance chips. For an EV she'd see chargers; as a petrol driver she sees
> the route summary."

---

## Step 2 — Discover with personalization (35 seconds)

> "Priya remembers her kids are hungry. She taps the **Discover tab**.
>
> At the top, because the active route matches a curated corridor, a **Hidden
> Gems carousel** appears: 'Mahalaxmi Sweets, Lonavla — original chikki since
> 1908', 'Rajmachi Viewpoint — sweeping Sahyadri views', 'German Bakery, Pune'.
> These are hand-picked editorial recommendations, not Google results.
>
> Below that is the **Smart Intelligence Grid** — 16 categories. She taps
> **Restaurants**.
>
> Three things make this list smart:
>
> The sort defaults to **'Best Match'** with a ✨ icon — driven by our
> personalised ranker, not raw distance.
>
> A **VIEW MODE** bar at the top shows four chips: Standard, Family,
> Women-Safe, Bike. She taps **Family** — the list instantly trims to
> family-friendly places and the count shows '12 of 24'.
>
> Every tile has a **source badge** — Official, Community, Curated, or
> Unverified — and a star rating from real community pulses."

---

## Step 3 — POI detail + Why we recommend (25 seconds)

> "She taps the top restaurant. A bottom sheet slides up with photos, address,
> fact pills, and the highlight:
>
> A **'Why we recommend this' card** explaining the rank in plain language:
>
> - 4.6 stars from 320 reviews
> - Open now
> - Matches your family mode
>
> Three reasons, no jargon, no math. Below that, an **'Open in Maps'** button,
> a **Community Reports section** with reliability score, and a **'Share a
> quick pulse'** button so she can leave a rating after the meal."

---

## Step 4 — The trip experience (45 seconds)

> "Back to Plan. Priya taps **'Start this trip'**. The app jumps to the Trip
> tab where she sees a live dashboard with elapsed time ticking, pause/resume,
> and end-trip buttons.
>
> Now the predictive engine takes over. As Priya drives:
>
> Around the 60 km mark, the **Ghat alert** fires — a yellow banner: 'Bhor
> Ghat in 12 km — about 10 km of winding ghat road. Drive slow, use lower
> gears on descents.' This banner auto-dismisses after 8 seconds because it's
> a warning, not a critical alert.
>
> When she opens the Fuel category now, the source badge says **'Ahead on
> your route'** — it only shows fuel stops she can still reach, not ones
> she's driven past.
>
> If she were driving at 11 PM, the **Night alert** would suggest the nearest
> hotel within 45 km.
>
> If she'd been driving 3 hours non-stop, the **Fatigue alert** would remind
> her to take a 15-minute break.
>
> Every alert respects a 20-minute cooldown — no spam, but a renewed
> condition can re-fire."

---

## Step 5 — The trip ends (25 seconds)

> "Priya reaches Pune. She taps **End Trip**, confirms.
>
> The completed summary shows duration, distance, stations passed, cost. An
> **ETA vs Actual banner** says 'On time vs. estimate' in green.
>
> She taps **'Share this trip'** — the OS share sheet pops up with a
> formatted text: '🚗 Mumbai → Pune, 150 km, ETA ~3h 12m, Fuel ~₹1,180,
> Tolls ~₹350' plus a Google Maps URL anyone can tap. Three taps to
> WhatsApp it to her husband.
>
> The trip is automatically saved to **Trip History** in the Profile tab —
> locally on her device, no GPS trails uploaded to any server."

---

## The closing (20 seconds)

> "Next Friday Priya wants to make the same trip. She opens Trip History,
> taps last week's entry, taps **'Plan again'** — the Plan screen pre-fills
> with Mumbai → Pune, same vehicle, same preferences. One tap to re-analyze.
>
> That's the loop. **Plan it. Drive it. The app watches the road ahead.
> Share it. Repeat it.**
>
> Every smart signal — weather, traffic, ghats, fatigue, charging gaps,
> community trust, personalised ranking — works automatically. Priya never
> opened a settings menu, never typed a search query, never read a manual.
>
> That's TripPlus."

## Tips for delivering this

**Pace yourself with the trip metaphor.** Don't list features — narrate the
drive. Each feature appears when Priya would actually need it.

**Use names, not codes.** "Bhor Ghat", "Mumbai → Pune", "Mahalaxmi Sweets" —
these make the demo feel local. Avoid saying "PoiRanker" or "P2-001" out loud.

**Land the big ideas three times:**

1. "Predictive, not reactive" (during ghat alert)
2. "Personalised, transparent" (during Why we recommend)
3. "Local-first, no surveillance" (during trip history mention)

**Emphasis points for impact:**

- Weather + traffic are **live, not static** — Open-Meteo + Google Directions live duration
- Personalization is **transparent** — every recommendation can be explained
- Trip history is **on-device only** — no location surveillance
- Community pulses **work offline** with a submit queue
- Modes are **one tap** — the whole app changes lens

## Variant scripts

### 90-second elevator version

Plan (10s) → Best Match list with Family mode (15s) → Why we recommend (15s)
→ Start trip + ghat alert (25s) → End + share (15s) → Closing (10s)

### 5-minute extended version

Add the following after Step 4:

- A **community pulse submission** (show the tri-state tag UI — Baby-friendly,
  Felt safe for women, Clean — tap to set ✓/✗, tap again to clear)
- **Offline banner** (toggle airplane mode mid-demo; amber banner slides in;
  toggle back; banner slides away)
- **Map clustering** (open a category → switch to map view → zoom out to see
  green count badges form; zoom in and they expand back into individual pins)
- **Conflict timeline** on a POI with mixed recent ratings (newest-first
  chronological list with colour-coded condition dots)

### Voice/screen-only version (no narrator)

Open the app, repeat the flow above slowly with on-screen captions
highlighting each surface. Pin a tooltip overlay on each numbered step so the
viewer can self-pace.

## Pre-demo checklist

Before going live, confirm:

- [ ] `.env` has `GOOGLE_MAPS_API_KEY` and `OPEN_CHARGE_MAP_API_KEY`
- [ ] Device has internet (to fetch weather + traffic)
- [ ] You're signed in (so the share sheet has a real user identifier)
- [ ] At least one trip is in history (so "Plan again" actually does something)
- [ ] Vehicle is set to **Petrol** for this demo (so fuel cost cards render)
- [ ] Profile preferences have **Family Mode** toggled on once at any point
      (so the Family chip change feels personalised)
- [ ] You've planned **Mumbai → Pune** at least once recently (so the
      corridor cache hits and Hidden Gems renders without delay)
- [ ] Mute notifications from other apps before the demo
- [ ] Mirror your phone to the projector via QuickTime / scrcpy
- [ ] Hold the screenshot of the ghat alert as a backup if live driving isn't
      possible during the demo

Good demos hide the loading spinners. Two-minute prep beats a fumbled live
moment.
